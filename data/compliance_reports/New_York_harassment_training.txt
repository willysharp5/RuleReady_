Covered Employers
New York State Law requires that employers of one or more employees must conduct sexual harassment prevention training for all employees each year. New York City Law also has training requirements for employers. To learn about NYC requirements, please see the [Frequently Asked Questions](https://www.nyc.gov/site/cchr/law/sexual-harassment-training-faqs.page).
- In NYC, employers **must** keep a record of employees’ training and must retain records for three years.
- Your employer should tell you before you take the training how they want to receive the certificate.

What Should Employers Do?
New York State Law requires that employers of one or more employees must conduct sexual harassment prevention training for all employees each year. New York City Law also has training requirements for employers. To learn about NYC requirements, please see the [Frequently Asked Questions](https://www.nyc.gov/site/cchr/law/sexual-harassment-training-faqs.page).
The Commission has developed an online training that will satisfy both the New York State and New York City sexual harassment prevention training requirements.
- In NYC, employers **must** keep a record of employees’ training and must retain records for three years.
- If you are taking this training on a desktop computer or laptop, you will be able to print or save the certificate to your computer. At the end of the training, **_you must click the “SAVE” button first_** in order to generate your certificate of completion.

North Dakota Crime Victim / Domestic Violence Leave

Overview
In North Dakota, domestic violence “includes physical harm, bodily injury, sexual activity compelled by physical force, assault, or the infliction of fear of imminent physical harm, bodily injury, sexual activity compelled by physical force, or assault, not committed in self-defense, on the complaining family or household members.” ( [N.D.C.C. § 14-07.1-01(2)](https://www.legis.nd.gov/cencode/t14c07-1.pdf#nameddest=14-07p1-01))
A Domestic Violence Protection Order, or DVPO, is a civil order from a North Dakota state district court. A DVPO requires the person committing domestic violence to stop and to leave the victim(s) listed on the order alone.  A violation of a DVPO is a criminal matter.  A first violation is a class A misdemeanor, a second or subsequent violation is a class C felony.

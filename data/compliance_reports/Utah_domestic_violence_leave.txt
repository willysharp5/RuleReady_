Utah Crime Victim / Domestic Violence Leave

Overview
An employee who receives retirement benefits and accrues leave. ( [Contact HR](https://sites.google.com/utcourts.gov/courts-hr/contact-hr) if you don’t know if you are qualified)
An immediate family member is defined as an employee’s parent, spouse, child under the age of 18, or sibling or an individual who the employee claims as a dependent for state or federal income tax purposes.

Covered Employers
No, an employer may not interfere with a qualified employee’s right to use Safe Leave or retaliate against them for using it.

Covered Employees
Safe leave is a type of paid administrative leave available to qualified employees or an employee's immediate family member who has been a victim of domestic violence, sexual assault, stalking, or human trafficking. The leave allows up to 40 hours of paid leave in one calendar year. In accordance with [SB 174](https://le.utah.gov/~2024/bills/static/SB0174.html), safe leave went into effect on January 1, 2025. Additional information can be found in HR policy ( [HR07-23](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1I4Ou9LLg3RR0TrRuxKmLSFM4gc5RtfvaJfKki19TTU4/edit?tab=t.0)).
Any qualified employee who is a direct victim of domestic violence, sexual assault, stalking, or human trafficking or an employee whose immediate family member is a victim of an incident described above.

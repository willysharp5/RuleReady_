New York Paid Family / Medical Leave (PFML)

Overview
In 2016, New York enacted the nation’s strongest and most comprehensive Paid Family Leave policy so working families would not have to choose between caring for their loved ones and risking their economic security.
Paid Family Leave provides eligible employees job-protected, paid time off to: [bond](https://paidfamilyleave.ny.gov/paid-family-leave-bonding) with a newly born, adopted or fostered child, [care](https://paidfamilyleave.ny.gov/paid-family-leave-family-care) for a family member with a serious health condition, or [assist](https://paidfamilyleave.ny.gov/paid-family-leave-military-families) loved ones when a spouse, domestic partner, child or parent is deployed abroad on active military service. Paid Family Leave may also be available in some situations when an employee or their minor, dependent child is under an order of quarantine or isolation due to [COVID-19](https://paidfamilyleave.ny.gov/COVID19).

Covered Employees
Paid Family Leave provides eligible employees job-protected, paid time off to: [bond](https://paidfamilyleave.ny.gov/paid-family-leave-bonding) with a newly born, adopted or fostered child, [care](https://paidfamilyleave.ny.gov/paid-family-leave-family-care) for a family member with a serious health condition, or [assist](https://paidfamilyleave.ny.gov/paid-family-leave-military-families) loved ones when a spouse, domestic partner, child or parent is deployed abroad on active military service. Paid Family Leave may also be available in some situations when an employee or their minor, dependent child is under an order of quarantine or isolation due to [COVID-19](https://paidfamilyleave.ny.gov/COVID19).
As of January 1, 2025, workers in New York who are pregnant may also have additional paid time off available to them for doctors’ appointments, procedures, or other types of prenatal care. This paid prenatal leave is part of New York’s paid sick leave program, which is separate from New York State Paid Family Leave. Please visit the [NYS Paid Prenatal Leave webpage](https://www.ny.gov/programs/new-york-state-paid-prenatal-leave) for more information on this benefit.

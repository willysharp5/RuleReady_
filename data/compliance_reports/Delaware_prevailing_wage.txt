Delaware Prevailing Wage Laws (public contracts)

Overview
Prevailing rates must be paid on new construction projects costing more than $500,000 and on alteration, repair, renovation, rehabilitation, demolition, or reconstruction projects costing more than $45,000. For a project to be covered by the law, the State or any subdivision thereof must be a party to the public works contract; and, the State must have appropriated any part of the funds.
The Department of Labor determines the classification of workers and the type of construction on state-funded construction projects. Wage rates are established by an annual survey conducted by the Department.
